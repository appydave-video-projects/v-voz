## vOz / SongShine Visual Style Guide Brief (v1.4)

### 1. **Core Visual Philosophy**

This project blends hand-drawn warmth with narrative restraint. It is inspired by Studio Ghibli, 90s RPGs, and storybook art ‚Äî emotional but grounded, metaphorical without fantasy excess. The visuals support a quiet, reflective story about compassion, dignity, and rising above cruelty.

---

### 2. **Historical & Cultural Setting**

The story is set in a **fictional French village during the 1930s**, a time marked by hardship and quiet resilience.
The village feels humble and handmade ‚Äî cobblestone streets, red-tiled roofs, wooden shutters, painted signage.
The baker is possibly of **Greek-French heritage**, running a small shop as a trusted but slightly outsider figure.
The time and setting justify the boy‚Äôs homelessness, the absence of safety nets, and the grim sentence passed by a detached justice system.

This hybrid context allows for grounded realism with soft poetic touches and echoes of European illustrated books.

### 6. **Texture & Linework**

* Visible hand-drawn texture or watercolor grain
* Clean but **not** vector-perfect lines
* Layered depth using subtle outlines + color blending
* Painterly, never plastic
* All environments, props, and characters share a unified semi-anime, painterly aesthetic. This ensures visual coherence across scenes. Textures remain hand-rendered in feel; nothing should appear digitally sterile or overly polished.

---

### 7. **Lighting & Atmosphere**

* Lighting evolves with emotional tone across scenes
* **Warm golden light** for bakery and safe spaces
* **Cooler tones** in courtroom and jail scenes
* **Amber or radiant glow** in transformation/final moments
* Dust and particles catch sunlight indoors
* Shadows are soft-edged, deep but gentle
